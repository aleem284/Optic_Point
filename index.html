INTRODUCTION

Seeing someone wearing eyeglasses today is just as common as seeing a person wearing a scarf during the winter time. Glasses are a tool that provides vision to most people, regardless of prescription or eye shape. But it wasn’t always this way.

Optic Point was Established in 1996, we have variety of glasses, sun glasses and contact lenses. We provide Computerized Eye Testing by a professional Eye Specialist.

You’ve found the perfect frames for your new pair of glasses, but you now have to choose your lenses. You might find yourself wondering what the different types of lenses for glasses are. Finding the right lenses for you is just as important as getting frames that fit your face and lifestyle.
So, what are the different types of lenses for glasses?

  • Vision lenses:   It made for one, consistent focal power to correct a single vision condition.
  • Progressive lenses:  It can correct more than one vision condition, with the focal power “progressing” from top to bottom without a visible line separating them.
  • Concave lenses:  It used to treat near-sightedness, or myopia. The lens shape is concave (thicker at the edge, thinner in the middle) to bend rays outwards.
  • Plus lenses: It used to treat far-sightedness, or hyperopia. The lens shape is convex (thicker in the middle, thinner at the edge) to bend rays inwards.
  • Cylindrical lenses:  It designed for astigmatisms, or when the cornea of the eye is oval, rather than round. A cylindrical lens is the most common way to correct astigmatism.
